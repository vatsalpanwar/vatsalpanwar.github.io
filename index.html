Hello! I am a 4th year PhD student (expecting to defend in Fall 2021) at Anton Pannekoek Institute, University of Amsterdam. I am interested in pursuing some of the fundamental the questions about the formation and evolution of planetary systems through the lens of atmospheric characterization of exoplanets. I have been leading a survey comprising of 350 hours of observations from Gemini/GMOS to measure the low-resolution optical transmission spectra of a sample of transiting gas-giant exoplanets spanning a wide range of system parameters. I am also working on characterizing young exoplanets transiting bright stars using observations from ground-based high-resolution spectrographs and the Hubble Space Telescope. You can find more about my research here. I am also a member of Astrobites, an international graduate student collaboration contributing to a daily astrophysical literature journal that summarizes peer reviewed Astronomy research for undergraduate students in the physical sciences. You can read the Astrobites posts contributed my me here.
